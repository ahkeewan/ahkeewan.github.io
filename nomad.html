Deleuze and Guattari's concept of nomadism is a direct response to what they call "the State" or "sedentary thought." In their view, traditional Western philosophy and societal structures are characterized by an inclination toward static categorizations, hierarchies, and fixed identities. They argue that such sedentary thinking restricts our ability to adapt, evolve, and flourish in a world marked by constant change.
